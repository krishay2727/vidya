How this project works ▾

Energy harvesting

The VAWT (Vertical Axis Wind Turbine) spins from passing vehicle wake and ambient wind, turning a small generator. A voltage divider and an ACS712 current sensor measure exactly how much electricity that motion produces.

Real vs. apparent power

Real power (P = V×I) is the actual work being done, in watts. Apparent power (S = Vrms×Irms) matters on AC systems where voltage and current can drift out of phase. This rig is DC, so the two are equal here — the dashboard shows both so the distinction is visible.

Energy over time

Power is a snapshot; energy is power accumulated over time (E = ∫P dt, in watt-hours). The dashboard integrates every reading as it arrives to show total energy harvested this session.

Measuring RPM

A magnet on the rotor sweeps past a hall-effect sensor once per revolution. The firmware just reports each pass; the browser times the gap between passes and converts it to RPM with 60000 / Δt.

Automatic streetlight

A digital LDR module reports DARK or BRIGHT. The firmware drives a streetlight LED directly from that reading — no dashboard logic required — and reports the change so it can be shown here too.

Environment context

A DHT11 sensor reports ambient temperature and humidity, useful context for how weather affects both turbine output and traffic patterns near the site.
